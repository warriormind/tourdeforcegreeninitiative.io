Project Information

  • Category: Urban Greening
  • Location: Mumbai, India
  • Project Date: March 2022 - Ongoing
  • Community Partners: 5 local schools, 3 resident associations
  • Volunteers: 250+ youth participants

Transforming Urban Spaces Through Community Action

The Urban Forest Project converted a 2-acre vacant lot in central Mumbai into a thriving green space with native trees, walking paths, and educational areas. What began as a barren concrete space is now home to over 500 trees and shrubs, providing much-needed greenery in one of the city's most densely populated areas.

Our youth volunteers led the design process, working with urban planners and ecologists to create a space that serves both environmental and community needs. The project includes:

  • Native tree species selected for urban resilience
  • Rainwater harvesting system
  • Community composting area
  • Educational signage about local ecosystems
  • Accessible walking paths

The space now serves as both a carbon sink and a community gathering place, with regular environmental education workshops and volunteer days to maintain the forest.
